Matthias Zach - Mathematical Researcher

I graduated in 2017 from the Leibniz Universitaet Hannover with a Dr. rer. nat. in Mathematics with a thesis on Topological Invariants of Isolated Determinantal Singularities.

I am currently employed at the RPTU Kaiserslautern as part of the SFB-TRR 195 on Symbolic Tools in Mathematics and their Application where I am one of the core developers for the OSCAR software package for Algebraic schemes; see also our chapter on Elliptic fibrations on Vinberg's most algebraic K3-surface (joint with Simon Brandhorst) for the OSCAR book or my talk at BIRS in 2024 about our joint work in progress.

See here for my GitHub page. You can reach me under lastname at rptu dot de.

Research interests

  • Complex analytic singularities: Deformations and their topology, computation of (topological) invariants, characteristic classes.
  • Singularities of mappings: Disentanglements, alternating homology, perverse sheaves.
  • Algorithmic commutative algebra: applications to Algebraic Geometry at scale, computation of global geometric invariants, (sheaf) cohomology.
